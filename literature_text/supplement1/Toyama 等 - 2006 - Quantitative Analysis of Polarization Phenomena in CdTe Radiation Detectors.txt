Quantitative Analysis of Polarization Phenomena in CdTe Radiation Detectors
Hiroyuki T OYAMA , Akira H IGA, Masaaki Y AMAZATO , Takehiro M AEHAMA ,
Ryoichi O HNO1 and Minoru T OGUCHI
Faculty of Engineering, University of the Ryukyus, 1 Senbaru, Nishihara, Okinawa 903-0213, Japan
1Acrorad Co., Ltd., 13-23 Suzaki, Uruma, Okinawa 904-2234, Japan
(Received March 7, 2006; accepted July 14, 2006; published online November 8, 2006)
Polarization phenomena in a Schottky-type CdTe radiation detector were studied. We evaluated the distribution of electric
ﬁeld in a biased CdTe detector by measuring the progressive change of Schottky barrier lowering with time. The parameters of
deep acceptors such as detrapping time, concentration, and the depth of the energy level were quantitatively evaluated. In the
case of applying the conventional model of charge accumulation, the obtained result shows that the CdTe bulk is never
undepleted. We modiﬁed the charge accumulation model by taking account of the occupation state of the deep acceptor level.
When a modiﬁed model is applied, the time that the depletion width in the bulk begins to diminish closely ﬁts the time that the
photopeak position begins to shift in radiation measurements. In this paper, we present a distribution of electric ﬁeld during
biasing and a simple method for the evaluation of the parameters of deep acceptors in CdTe bulk.
[DOI: 10.1143/JJAP.45.8842]
KEYWORDS: CdTe, X-ray and /C13-ray detector, polarization phenomena, Schottky barrier, deep acceptor
1. Introduction
Cadmium telluride (CdTe) is a compound semiconductor
with a large band-gap energy ( Eg ¼ 1:47 eV) and a high
atomic number (Cd: 48, Te: 52). Therefore, CdTe oﬀers
room-temperature operation and a high absorption eﬃciency
for application as X- and /C13-ray detectors. These devices have
been used in a wide range of applications, such as medical
1)
and industrial imaging, 2) and X- and /C13-ray astronomy. 3) The
energy resolution of X- and /C13-ray detectors is one of the
important factors for practical use, but it strongly depends on
the leakage current of the detector. To suppress the leakage
current, Schottky-type (or diode-type) CdTe detectors have
been fabricated.
4,5) As a result, high energy resolutions such
as the FWHMs of the 59.5, 122, and 662 keV photopeaks as
small as 1.5, 2.1, and 10 keV, respectively, were obtained
from the detectors.
5)
However, Schottky-type CdTe detectors suﬀer from an
instability with time under bias voltage. 6,7) For the practical
application of CdTe detectors, the time stability of the
detectors is one of the most important issues. This instability
phenomenon is generally termed as a polarization phenom-
enon and characterized by a progressive degradation of
energy resolution and a shifting of the photopeak toward low
energy with time after applying the bias voltage. To our
knowledge, the term ‘ ‘polarization’ ’ had been used to express
the polarization of the space charge distribution which
causes the disturbance of electric ﬁeld in crystal-type
detectors.
8) In the present day, the term ‘ ‘polarization’ ’ is
used for the time instability phenomena in semiconductor
detectors. Earlier works revealed that polarization phenom-
ena result from the accumulation of negative charge on a
deep acceptor level during the application of bias volt-
age.
9–12) As a result, the distribution of electric ﬁeld in CdTe
bulk varies with time. In fact, the polarization phenomena
were observed in several kinds of radiation detectors using
compound semiconductors such as HgI
2,13) PbI2,14) and
TlBr.15) Deep acceptors can be evaluated using techniques
such as deep-level transient spectroscopy (DLTS) and
thermally stimulated current (TSC) measurements, but these
methods are rather complicated.
In this paper, we present a method for a quantitative
evaluation of the parameters of the deep acceptors (detrap-
ping time /C28, concentration N
T and energy level ET /C0 EV)o f
a Schottky-type CdTe detector through the measurement of
Schottky barrier lowering, on the basis of the conventional
model of charge accumulation. Additionally, we modiﬁed
the charge accumulation model by taking account of the
occupation state of the deep acceptor level. The time that the
width of a depletion layer becomes smaller than the detector
thickness was compared with the time that a photopeak shift
occurs in energy spectra.

4.2 Modiﬁcation of charge accumulation model
In the conventional model, it is assumed that all the deep
acceptor levels are empty before applying the bias voltage,
and will be occupied by electrons after applying the bias
voltage. However, the occupation of the deep levels is
actually determined by the Fermi–Dirac function. Assuming
the zero-temperature approximation, a deep acceptor level is
empty if it lies above the Fermi level E
F and will be
occupied by electrons below EF [Fig. 5(a)]. For the case of
p-type CdTe, a deep acceptor level can cross the Fermi level
when the bands are bent by the formation of a Schottky
contact. The intersection point between a deep acceptor level
and the Fermi level is symbolized by /C21. If the barrier height
at zero bias /C30b0 is larger than a deep acceptor level in the
vicinity of the Schottky contact, the deep acceptor level is
supposed to be situated below E
F. From our result,
/C30b0 ¼ 0:973 eV is larger than a deep acceptor level, imply-
ing that the deep levels in the region of /C21 are already
occupied before the application of the bias voltage. If a
reverse bias is applied, the Fermi level is split into quasi-
Fermi levels for electrons and holes. As shown by Rhoderick
and Williams,
18) it is a very good approximation to assume
that the hole quasi-Fermi level EFp is horizontal throughout
the depletion region and coincides with the Fermi level in
the semiconductor, while the electron quasi-Fermi level E
Fe
is also horizontal and coincides with the Fermi level in the
metal [Fig. 5(b)]. When EFp is greater than the deep acceptor
levels, they begin to be occupied at a rate with a detrapping
time /C28, and negative space charge is accumulated.
According to this model, the potential V and the electric
ﬁeld E must be obtained by solving Poisson’s equations in
two regions.

5. Conclusions
We investigated polarization phenomena in a Schottky-
type CdTe radiation detector through the measurement of
Schottky barrier lowering. On the basis of the conventional
model of charge accumulation, the parameters of deep
acceptors such as detrapping time /C28, concentration N
T, and
the depth of the energy level ET /C0 EV were extracted. It
is considered that the obtained energy level arises from
cadmium vacancies in the crystal. However, the simulated
distribution of electric ﬁeld shows that the CdTe bulk will
never be undepleted, which is inconsistent with the
observation of photopeak shifts in spectra. On the other
hand, the modiﬁed model corrects the parameters to /C28 ¼
836 min, N
T ¼ 4:56 /C2 1012 cm/C0 3, and ET /C0 EV ¼ 0:69 eV.
In the modiﬁed model, the distribution of electric ﬁeld
suggests that the depletion width begins to diminish at a
certain time. It was found that the time that the depletion
width becomes less than the thickness of the bulk coincides
with the time that the photopeak position begins to shift.
Our results show that the modiﬁed model of charge
accumulation represents an accurate distribution of electric
ﬁeld in CdTe bulk. In addition, our method for the
evaluation of deep acceptors is simple as we only have to
measure the current–voltage characteristics of a detector. We
believe that this method will become useful in the ﬁelds of
crystal growth and device fabrication not only for CdTe but
also other compound semiconductors.
