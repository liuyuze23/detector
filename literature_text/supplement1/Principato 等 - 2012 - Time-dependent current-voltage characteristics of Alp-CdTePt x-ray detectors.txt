F . Principato,a) G. Gerardi, A. A. T urturici, and L. Abbene
Dipartimento di Fisica, Universit /C19a di Palermo, Viale delle Scienze, Edi’¼ücio 18, 90128 Palermo, Italy
(Received 26 June 2012; accepted 9 October 2012; published online 7 November 2012)
Current-voltage (I-V) characteristics of Schottky Al/p-CdTe/Pt detectors were investigated in dark
and at different temperatures. CdTe detectors with Al rectifying contacts, very appealing for high
resolution x-ray and gamma ray spectroscopy, suffer from bias-induced polarization phenomena
which cause current increasing with the time and severe worsening of the spectroscopic
performance. In this work, we studied the time-dependence of the I-V characteristics of the
detectors, both in reverse and forward bias, taking into account the polarization effects. The I-V
measurements, performed at different time intervals between the application of the bias voltage and
the measurement of the current, and the measured current transients show as the electrical instability
manifests itself even in time intervals shorter (i.e., <1 s) than those are necessary to make evident
the degradation of the spectroscopic properties. The results point out as this time interval is a critical
parameter for correct investigations on the electrical properties of these devices. Although the
detectors are typically classi’¼üed as Schottky-type detectors, we ’¼ünd that the thermionic emission is
not the dominant transport mechanism, except at very low bias voltage values. Finally, the Schottky
barrier height of the Al/p-CdTe contact was extracted by measuring the contact resistance at zero
bias.

I. INTRODUCTION
The appealing potentialities of cadmium telluride
(CdTe) and cadmium zinc telluride (CdZnTe) detectors for
x-ray and gamma ray spectroscopy are now widely recog-
nized.1ŌĆō3 Among the traditional semiconductor spectrome-
ters, CdTe detectors show high detection ef’¼üciency and good
room temperature performance and are well suited for the
development of compact and portable detection systems. 4
Beside their successful astrophysical applications, 1,2 a great
effort has recently been devoted to the development of CdTe
detectors for medical applications, such as x-ray spectros-
copy in mammography
5,6 and energy resolved photon count-
ing detectors in diagnostic medicine (mammography and
computed tomography). 7ŌĆō9 High-resolution CdTe detectors
are generally fabricated with rectifying contacts, working as
diodes with indium (In) as the anode electrode on a p-type
CdTe wafer and platinum (Pt) as the cathode. 1 Rectifying
contacts ensure low leakage currents, if compared with an
ohmic electrode con’¼üguration (Pt/CdTe/Pt), allowing high
bias voltage operation and optimum charge collection. Due
to the dif’¼üculties of dividing an In electrode into pixels, alu-
minum (Al) has been recently found to be a good alternative
electrode material for diode detectors.
10ŌĆō12 In addition to low
leakage currents comparable to those of standard In/CdTe/Pt
detectors, Al/CdTe/Pt detectors allow pixellization of anodes
and then the possibility to realize electron-collecting-type
diode detectors.
As well known, CdTe detectors with rectifying contacts
(Al and In) suffer from instability with time under bias
voltage, causing dramatic worsening in the spectroscopic
performance.
13ŌĆō15 Several studies demonstrated as this time
instability in CdTe detectors, known as polarization, is
mainly related to the accumulation of negative charge on
deep acceptor levels during the application of the bias volt-
age (charge accumulation model, CAM).
13ŌĆō19 Polarization
produces a progressive increasing of the reverse current with
the time which causes a severe degradation of the energy re-
solution of the detectors. Studies on polarization phenomena
in Schottky CdTe detectors are typically performed through
an electrical approach.
17ŌĆō21 At the end of 2006, Toyama and
co-workers17 presented some results on the physical proper-
ties of deep acceptors and the Schottky barrier height (Al/
CdTe) by measuring the current voltage curves of Al/CdTe/
Pt detectors and on the basis of the CAM model. In this
work, the time dependence of the leakage current is consid-
ered only for long times (the order of minutes). In Ref. 17,
the extraction of the barrier height of the Al/CdTe contact is
obtained from the straight line intercept of a semi-log for-
ward I-V plot at zero bias, supposing that the I-V curve at
the starting time (t ┬╝ 0) is not affected by the polarization. In
Ref. 22, the time instability of the leakage current of
Schottky CdTe detectors was characterized by measurements
in a 30 s =100 h time scale after the bias application. In
Ref. 19, the polarization Schottky CdTe detectors were stud-
ied in simulated structures, under dark bias, solving drift-
diffussion and Poisson equations numerically. The results of
the current transient after biasing for Al/CdTe/Pt and In/
CdTe/Pt detectors were obtained with a starting time of the
order of a few seconds and shorter times are not taken into
account in order to speed up the simulation time.

on Schottky Al/p-CdTe/Pt detectors pointing out the

IV . DISCUSSION
The main result of the electrical characterization of the
investigated Al/p-CdTe/Pt x-ray detectors is the time de-
pendence of the I-V curves, even for short time intervals
(<1 s) between the setting time of the source voltage and the
start time of the current measurement. Indeed, the increase
with time of current related to polarization effects is a well
known critical issue of these Schottky CdTe detectors, con-
trary to ohmic detectors, but usually this effect on the electri-
cal characterization was taken into account only for long
times (i.e., >1 s).
15,17,18,20,21,31 This time dependence occurs
both in forward and reverse bias, except at low voltages
(<0:1 V). Generally, the polarization is ascribed to ionization
of deep acceptor levels derived from defects (e.g., Cadmium
vacancies), which generate a negative space charge near the
anode contact after the application of the bias voltage. This
space charge screens out the bias and the dead layer is
formed near the cathode.
13,14,17,19 Hence, the transport
mechanism is dominated in a complicated way by the charge
trapping and de-trapping from the deep levels. We noted that
at low bias the polarization does not manifest itself and that
the dependence of the contact resistance on the temperature
obeys Eq. (2) valid for the thermionic emission model.
Hence, the extracted barrier height values in the range
0:71ŌĆō0:73 eV for the investigated samples concerns the bar-
rier for the holes at the Al/CdTe contact. These values are
comparable with those obtained by Ref. 26 in Al/p-CdTe
diodes, although they obtained two values for the barrier
height, 0.69 eV when the thermionic emission model is sup-
posed valid and 0.74 eV with the diffusion model and the ca-
pacitance method. Toyama
21 found for Al/p-CdTe/Pt
detectors, produced by Acrorad, values of the Al/CdTe bar-
rier height in the range 0 :2ŌĆō0:6 eV as a function of the CdTe
surface He plasma treatment. In this case, the method to
extract the barrier height is based on the assumption that the
reverse current, even at high bias voltage, is governed by the
thermionic emission model. Moreover, Toyama does not
taken into account the time dependence of the I-V curves for
short times.

V . CONCLUSION
The electrical characterization of Al/p-CdTe/Pt Schottky
detectors, fabricated by Acrorad for X and gamma ray spec-
troscopy applications, performed at different temperatures
reveals that the current, both in reverse and forward bias, is
not only dependent on the temperature but also on the time
between the setting time of the source voltage and the start-
ing time of the current measurement. The current-voltage
characteristics show the rectifying behavior of these detec-
tors and that the forward current is governed by the time-
dependent SCLC mechanism.
The reverse I-V curves exhibit as well time dependence,
even at low temperatures. This time dependence of the
reverse current occurs in the whole investigated time scale
1m sŌĆō1000 s, where the time evolution of current does not
allow to obtain steady-state I-V curves, which generally are
used to determine the transport mechanism. This behavior is
related to the polarization phenomenon, which is characteris-
tic for Schottky-type CdTe detectors, although usually the
emergence of the polarization is assumed to occur a few sec-
onds after the application of the bias voltage.
The time dependence of the I-V disappears for low bias
voltage ( <0:1 V), where the current is dominated by the
thermionic emission model. Indeed, the contact resistance
measured at different temperatures is consistent with that
predicted by the thermionic emission model and allow us to
extract the values of the Al/CdTe Schottky barrier height in
the range 0 :71ŌĆō0:73 eV.
