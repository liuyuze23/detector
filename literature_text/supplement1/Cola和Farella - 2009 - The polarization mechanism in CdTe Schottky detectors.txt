It is worth to note that two competing effects are at
work: the hole detrapping enhances the acceptor ionization
leading to an increase in the electric ’¼üeld at the anode; how-
ever, such increase causes a current increase which in turn
leads to an acceptor ionization decrease. Hence, the system
attains stationary conditions at a speci’¼üc acceptor ionization
level. A further evidence of the correlation between polariza-
tion and current transients comes from the very small current

In conclusion, exploiting the Pockel effect, we measured
the electric ’¼üeld pro’¼üles as a function of time and tempera-
ture. This allows the monitoring of the space charge dynam-
ics at the anode and the identifying of the mechanism leading
to the observed polarization effect. The electric ’¼üeld distri-
bution is found to be governed by the ionized deep acceptors,
which are bulk defects probably associated to native cad-
mium vacancies. The interface at the anode plays a funda-
mental role in controlling the ’¼éowing current and therefore
the extent of hole detrapping which is, ultimately, the cause
of the polarization effect in CdTe detectors.
